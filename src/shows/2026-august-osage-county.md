description: "A father who has vanished. A pill-popping mother. Three sisters
  harboring shady little secrets. When the large Weston family unexpectedly
  reunites after their father disappears, their Oklahoman family homestead
  explodes in a maelstrom of repressed truths and unsettling secrets. Mix in
  Violet, the drugged-up, scathingly acidic matriarch, and you’ve got a play
  that unflinchingly – and uproariously – exposes the dark side of the
  Midwestern American family. Pulitzer and Tony Award-winning, *August: Osage
  County* is a modern masterpiece."
directors_note: >-
  We have been talking about doing *August: Osage County* for several years now.
  “Let’s do a big, powerful actors’ and directors’ dream play,” we thought. But
  it needed a large and brave cast, a challenging set and also offered several
  technical difficulties, and every year we regretfully acknowledged that this
  was not the year for it. This past fall however, the stars aligned, the people
  came forward and here we are! Making a play which has won a Tony Award and
  then been made into an Oscar winning movie, also offers certain challenges,
  but our cast have risen to those challenges and made this play their own. Our
  spectacular set not only works well and efficiently, but also enhances the
  themes of this story. This is neither a light nor a comfortable story, but it
  is very human and familiar and often gut wrenching. We have been awed and
  delighted by the power and the compassion which our cast and crew have bought
  to this saga of a large, and dysfunctional, Southern family. It is our hope
  that you too will be moved and astonished. 


  I would like to thank Rob Rodgers for once more accepting the challenge and co-directing this difficult yet rewarding play. Rob and I have worked together as a great team, me directing the big picture and seeing the panoramic vision from the back, and annoying the actors with my talk about theme and vision, and Rob helping the actors conquer the physical and emotional demands of this script up front. We had frequent conferences, many fruitful discussions a lot of laughs and the occasional ‘talking each other down’! A great partnership! (I should add that the actors themselves continuously challenged themselves and us as they continued to grow and power up throughout the rehearsal process.) 


  Like all our plays, this is not a vehicle for one or two stars, but a collaboration where all voices are valued. Thank you to our cast and crew for their commitment and their input and their courage. 


  Judith Hamilton \

  Rob Rodgers
