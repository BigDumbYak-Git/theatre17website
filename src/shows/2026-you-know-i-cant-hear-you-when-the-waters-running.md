  *You Know I Can't Hear You When the Water's Running* is an evening of four short plays by Robert Anderson.


  **The Shock of Recognition**A quarrel over taste erupts between an earnest young dramatist and his matter-of-fact producer. The dispute centres on the opening moment of the playwright's new work — a husband emerges from the bathroom, jaybird naked, after his wife calls out to him: *"You know I can't hear you when the water's running."* The producer finds the moment unnecessary. The author insists it's essential. A job-hunting actor gets caught up in the debate — and eagerly begins to strip, demonstrating how he'd handle the role.


  **The Footsteps of Doves**After twenty-five years of marriage, a couple visit a furniture store to choose a new bed. Twin beds or a double? Their soft-spoken salesman offers little help — he has his own perspective on the matter. And then, uninvited, a young blonde woman joins the conversation. She wants a big bed. She's all alone.


  **I'll Be Home for Christmas**Lighter in tone but quietly serious at heart, this play follows two parents navigating the awkward territory of talking to their nearly-grown children about sex and relationships. A letter from their son — signalling that he's ready to leave the nest — brings an unexpected wave of emotion.


  **I'm Herbert**Two very old people sit on a porch in rocking chairs. They talk. They've each had a marriage or two — maybe a fling or three — but the details have grown hazy. They're not always sure which one the other one even is. They have no idea how funny they are.
